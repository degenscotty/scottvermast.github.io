UNREAL LEVEL

Creating models or assets in general is something I like, as I have mentioned before. Designing a level is whole other story..
During classes, they thought us how important it is to start from a blockout, see how the level feels as you walk through it and only start adding more detail if you feel like your blockout is final.

I had never designed any sort of level in my life and had no idea how to start. The theme for the level was supposed to be futuristic / spacy. So I started creating a room and filling it up piece by piece. Having no idea of what the rest of my level was gonna look like, I just focused on that one room.

Soon enough, I started getting ideas for the rest of my level and started looking at references like Star Wars and Star Trek.

Looking back at this project, I think I learned a lot. Creating / designing levels is not my strong suit but it did teach me a lot of fundamental processes that a game developer should be familiar with.
